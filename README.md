## About

- CS master's graduate.
- Currently working on measurement and control systems for quantum computers.
- Interested in systems that sit close to hardware, signals, automation, and engineering reality.
- Writing notes, thoughts, and technical records at [kanes.top](https://www.kanes.top).

| Area | What I care about |

| Quantum control | Measurement, control, timing, and system reliability |
| Systems engineering | Turning messy real-world constraints into usable software |
| Tooling | Automation, reproducibility, and workflows that reduce friction |
| Writing | Recording what I learn and what I still do not understand |

## Engineering Compass

| Principle | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Reality first | Hardware, signals, latency, and noise do not care about clean abstractions. |
| Small tools | Good automation removes repeated friction without hiding the system. |
| Reproducibility | A result is only useful when it can be explained, repeated, and trusted. |
| Writing | Notes turn vague understanding into something that can be debugged. |
